Prompt engineering is the practice of designing and refining inputs to large language models to elicit optimal responses. A well-crafted prompt provides clear instructions, context, constraints, and format requirements.

Techniques such as Few-Shot Prompting, where the model is given examples of desired outputs, can drastically improve task performance. Another crucial method is Chain-of-Thought (CoT) prompting, encouraging the model to explain its reasoning step-by-step before arriving at a final answer.

In the context of RAG systems, the generation prompt is highly structured. It must instruct the LLM to strictly base its answer only on the provided context retrieved from the database. A solid prompt also explicitly tells the AI what to do if the context lacks the answer, such as returning a polite "I don't know" instead of fabricating facts.
